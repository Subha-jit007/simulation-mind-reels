# Day 05 — There Are No Glitches In The Matrix

> [!abstract] Angle
> A perfect simulation predicts nothing different from reality — so glitch-hunting is doomed; an unfalsifiable claim is a mood, not a theory.

## 🎙️ Script 
People keep looking for glitches in the matrix. A déjà vu. A coincidence. A law of physics that seems too convenient. But here is the problem. A good simulation, by definition, behaves exactly like a real universe. If it didn't, it would be a bad one. So there is no experiment you can run, no glitch you can catch, that a real world wouldn't also produce. A claim that predicts nothing different from reality is not a theory. It is a mood. The simulation, if it exists, is perfect precisely because you can never tell.
